Introduction

About this Project

Imagine building your model without the stress of missing pieces or deciphering dense manuals. Gunplan transforms the experience, making it seamless and enjoyable. Our features, including AR part finders, animated assembly steps, and a vibrant community, empower builders at every stage. We directly addressed user frustration with vague instructions by creating intuitive AR-assisted tutorials.

Objectives

Imagine spending hours assembling a model, only to misplace a small but crucial part. Frustrating, right? For many Gundam builders, this is a common struggle. Gunplan was designed to eliminate these pain points and make model-building a seamless, frustration-free experience.

Here’s why:

  1. Confusing and Cluttered Visuals:

    Instruction manuals often span 40+ pages, packed with dense, unclear visuals.

  2. Vague and Inconsistent Guidance:

    Users must decipher cryptic icons and memorize complex color guides, leading to frustration and errors.

  3. Common Challenges:
    • Losing track of small parts.
    • Fear of breaking expensive components.
    • Uncertainty about using specialized tools.
  4. Time-Intensive Process:

    Building a model can take anywhere from 4 to 12 hours, often leaving users overwhelmed.

The Impact:

  • Beginners: New builders seeking clear guidance to simplify the process and build confidence.
  • Veteran Builders: Experienced hobbyists looking for tools to streamline complex builds and enhance efficiency.
  • Our Goal: Support all skill levels with intuitive features that make building enjoyable and hassle-free.

As the Lead Designer, I was responsible for overseeing the design process, ensuring the app's visual and functional aspects aligned with user needs. I collaborated with the team to create intuitive interfaces, interactive features, and engaging visual elements.

Research Process

View insight report
  1. Research Goals

    We aimed to understand why seasoned Gundam builders stay engaged while beginners often quit. Our goal was to uncover their frustrations, motivations, and the personal significance of Gundam building.

  2. Research Approach Participant Recruitment:
    • Recruited through Discord.
    • Pre-Interview Survey: Conducted via Google Forms for initial insights.
    • Interviews: Held on Zoom with structured questions, recorded with participant consent.
    • Transcription: Used Adobe Premiere for transcripts, manually refined for accuracy.
  3. Key Insights
    • Customization Hesitation: Builders want unique models but fear breaking parts or lack guidance.
    • Fragility Issues: Brittle parts and complex instructions discourage both beginners and veterans.
    • Community and Joy: Builders value community support and feel fulfilled by completing models.

Affinity Diagram & Empathy Map

View Diagram & Map

By employing Affinity Diagramming, we uncovered key themes such as the need for simplified instructions for beginners and flexible customization options for experienced users. We then delved deeper into user experiences using an Empathy Map. By segmenting users into beginner and expert categories, we gained insights into their thoughts, feelings, behaviors, and pain points. These valuable insights provided a solid foundation for our ideation and user-centered design processes.

Reflection

Project Experience

I initially proposed an Ikea app but was paired with a teammate working on Gunpla, an area unfamiliar to me. However, my passion for anime helped me connect with the community and develop a personal interest.

Challenges & Learning

User interviews were the biggest challenge due to my lack of knowledge about Gunpla building. Learning the terminology and techniques took time, but overcoming this allowed me to create a more user-centered solution.

Impact

This experience deepened my understanding of hobbies and the importance of empathy in UX design. I’m proud of the solution we built, which improves the Gunpla-building experience.
